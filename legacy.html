Legacy

    Thomas Edison is well known as one of the greatest inventors of all time. He had 1,093 patents and his contributions to society are enormous and they continue today. Edison had invented his version of the incandescent light bulb, phonograph, and an improved version of the stock ticker. His work helped make it possible for us to have electricity at home, listen to music and even watch movies. Thomas Edison was also a businessman and an entrepreneur who knew how to use his inventions to make money. The Edison Electric Company became one of the first giant corporations in the United States. Thomas Edison has been remembered through various ways. Although not an official US banknote, the Military Payment Certificate (MPC) was used to pay servicemen stationed overseas. It’s colored in green, yellow and purple. The obverse side features a large portrait of Thomas Edison on the right hand side. While on the left-hand side is an image of Benjamin Franklin holding a string with a metal key and a kite attached.
